Evaluating Sparse Autoencoders for Mechanistic Interpretability under Distribution Shift
Under Review · CVIT, IIIT Hyderabad
Authors: Sunayana Samavedam, Deepika Vemuri, Venkat Adithya Amula, Shreyas Premkhede, P. J. Narayanan, and Vineeth Balasubramanian
We investigate how Sparse Autoencoder (SAE) feature quality degrades when CLIP ViT models are adapted to out-of-distribution domains (medical imaging, remote sensing) via parameter-efficient fine-tuning. We introduce a novel metric, Distribution-Aware Monosemanticity Score (DAMS), to characterize SAE feature quality under shifts that standard metrics often conflate. Experiments reveal a systematic tension between downstream classification accuracy and feature-level interpretability under distribution shift.
Mechanistic Interpretability Sparse Autoencoders Distribution Shift
Toward a Taxonomy of Spatiotemporal Reasoning in Vision-Language Models
Under Review · CVIT, IIIT Hyderabad
Authors: Sunayana Samavedam and Adrian DeWynter
We survey and systematically organize the landscape of spatiotemporal reasoning evaluation in vision-language models (VLMs), identifying critical gaps across five reasoning categories: perception, spatial relations, temporal, dynamic, and compositional. Building on mechanistic evidence that VLMs encode object locations via linearly bound spatial IDs, we argue that most benchmarks measure accuracy without probing internal representations, and that cognitively grounded tests from neuroscience (mental rotation, multiple object tracking, perspective-taking) remain largely unadapted for VLM evaluation. We propose a structured taxonomy mapping evaluated versus unexplored capabilities, highlighting compositional spatiotemporal reasoning and temporal causality as the most undercharacterized failure modes.
Mechanistic Interpretability Spatio-Temporal Reasoning Vision-Language Models
